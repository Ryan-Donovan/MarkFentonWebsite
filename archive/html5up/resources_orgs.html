Resources for More Livable Communities: National Organizations

  • Active Living by Design: Extensive technical assistance & practical guidance on building healthy eating, active living settings.
  • Active Living Research, at UC San Diego: Definitive compilation of the research on active living designs, policies, and impacts.
  • Alliance for Biking and Walking: Great annual benchmarking report.
  • AmericaWalks: National pedestrian advocacy organization.
  • Bikes Belong Coalition: Coalition of bicycle industry retailers & suppliers supporting bike-friendly communities & policies, including a community grant program.
  • EPA Smart Growth program: Technical assistance, design & policy guidance, and funding opportunities.
  • Complete Streets: National coalition working for streets that work for pedestrians, bicyclists, and transits users of all ages, incomes, and abilities.
  • Centers for Disease Control and Prevention.: A site with tons of current data on health and physical activity, and promotional resources.
  • League of American Bicyclists: National advocacy group advancing the Bike Friendly Community, Business, & Campus programs..
  • Local Government Commission, Sacramento, CA: Technical guidance and practical planning and transportation guides, especially useful for local elected & appointed officials.
  • National Center for Bicycling and Walking: Organizes the biennial Pro Walk/Pro Bike conference, and sends out a info-laden monthly email newsletter.
  • Pedestrian and Bicycle Information Center: Comprehensive technical support & info including walkability and bike-ability checklists, design, & cost guides.
  • Project for Public Spaces: Terrific resources for inclusive and equitable community engagement & creation of livable, functional, & appealing communities.
  • Rails-to- Trails Conservancy, Washington, DC: Great help for trail and greenway advocates, including research supporting trails’ benefits.
  • Rivers and Trails Conservation Assistance, a program of the National Park Service: Provides technical planning, design, organizational support.
  • Safe Routes to School info: Nat’l Center for Safe Routes to School:
  • Safe Routes to School info: National Safe Routes to School Partnership,
  • Surface Transportation Policy Partnership: Publishes annual reports loaded with pedestrian activity and safety data.
  • Victoria Transportation Policy Institute, Victoria, British Columbia: Outstanding white papers, research evidence, and resources to support active transportation.
  • Walkscore.com: Get a score from 0-100 on the walkability of any address in North America!
